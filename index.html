Analysis and Simulation of COVID-19¶

India is facing a severe second wave of COVID-19 which is much worse than the first wave. It is spreading much faster. India has now surpassed U.S. in terms of daily COVID-19 cases. This paper aims to analyse the trend of COVID-19 and examine why second wave happened and why it is so bad by simulating a simple SEIR model. Which is a compartmental model based on 4 compartments Susceptible, Exposed, Infectious, Recovered.

p = [0.7,2.1,0.7,0.1]
s1 = SEIR(params_=p)
s1.rho=0.4
r1=s1.run(t_max=90,dt=0.1)
new_init = s1.vals_
s2 = SEIR(init_vals=new_init,params_=p)
s2.rho = 0.9
r2=s2.run(t_max=90,dt=0.1,reset=False)
r3=np.vstack((r1,r2))
s2.plot_var(r3[:,2],var_name='Infected')

This is the trend when lockdown is lifted and people stopped social distancing. Second wave is much higher than the first one as what we are seeing now in India.
